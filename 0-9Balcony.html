5/2/22: If I die (IF) please bury me next to a can of LaCroix Beach Plum (empty if I die under 50 and full if I die after 50).
The way Beach Plum LaCroix embodies my life is something so brilliant that I almost missed. Canned Apriocot juice from my childhood with the hummingbird on the label. Summer camp camping stealing plums from the Penske. The beach beckoning, a siren unto herself with distance industrial boobs too. The train rattling through my dreams into the present. The trip I didn't want to take and the beach where I felt the possiblity of peace again. Please bury me with my LaCroix Beach Plum. Oh, for the intersecting of my life to be canned up like this. The carbonationed slight flavor I scorned for flat oversaturated drinks that got me sugar high and left me feeling sticky and heavy. Who knew this would be the liquid of choice for my burial customization?

I feel I probably knew. When you find something that just intersects at all the right points in your life, it is hard not to believe some God or gods or chaos personified is grinning at you expectantly when these things happen. When you walk into a moment, leaving your kitchen-say, and the scene around you is definitively a dream you had, with the same inherent, inexplicable feelings from the dream that you are living through now, arrived to now.

That one poetry class really made me insufferable because I cannnot stop connecting two poems from that term. "You who never arrived" by Rilke and of course "Wish(2) by Kapil.

These two works are the two I return to. That my mind will summon on a sunny day as the leaves on the tree titter between realms.
