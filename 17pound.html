# old teddy bear

for some reason, he has springs in him that poke whoever cuddles him, but he is very sweet with his grape patches of fabric and button eyes

Nancy drew you velvet curtains
itching desert landscape
expanding dreams and painting frames
out and in, worlds take shape

Now can I find you by the wall?
Generations of moss
pull 'round you the bitter shawl-
Earthly wires to crisscross

Interwound in the ground, chin up;
Toothy grin to heaven
Biting bermuda buttercups.
tell me how to end it

I have seen your freckles before,
eeny meeny miny moe
cut him by the carnivore
Find me full of chloroform-
barreling in visions galore!


*if the first line of this poem doesn't make you audibly gasp, then i'm doing it wrong..

# tan teal wearing lass
she is a broken beloved beach themed barbie that smells of coconut and her feet don't fit in any of the shoes

Sitting out
Eye-level with the tip top of the trees
The ocean breeze blowing branches side to side
Flowing underwater
I watch through aquarium glass-
whether I am inside or outside
Watching
A spectacle of life displaced for my viewing entertainment...

Will it all repeat?
In the distance more trees writhe,
Oceans as if I could step out the window and fly
Or float
To be twirled around so aimlessly
To be a leaf hanged (man) to branch
Being pulled back, knowing soon I'll be forth

The Inevitability of Summer
Sweat trinkling down down.
Suffocation though I have not gone under
Unable to swim, unable to breath as I move through
Not elastic pulled like my counterparts,
Chemically strengthened still closing pipes,

Firefly trying to get in, or maybe out
I am unable to tell which side of the glass is which.


*the ending of this poem is unhinged, in a cringe way- but today i heard buzzing in bed and couldn't tell if the fucker was inside or outside buzzing away... confused
