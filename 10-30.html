Day 18
5/11/22: I always liked watching planes take off. I remember landing with my friend in California for camp, and we had to wait with the other saps who flew in for camp. Luckily, we were waiting with a very tall fellow I would later riff for too long about cults with. In my facing the window I screamed with glee seeing a plane take off so close. Naturally, my friend and tall dude had to bully me for getting so shocked by a plane taking off, but it just caught me off guard and filled me with such emotion. I think I told them it didn't seem real. How could a plane land or take flight looking like that? It made no sense, but filled me some wonder. How could I forget looking up at a seagull flying either right before or after that period of time, thinking there was no way. No way this seagull was flying like that. It looked like he was on a string. I felt for a moment the world around me dissolve into a million wires and screens, sparking and glitching out. The world, a mere series of coils and wires, screens and jellyfish-like conciousnesses bundles of cable chords and metal. I started writing a story on the beach that day with the seagull. I didn't get very far, but I got a prologue of a main character as a child in 1st grade (of course I was picturing my own first grade setting). Walking in line in the hall one day, the main character would fall behind the class stare intensely before seeing throughout the hall loose wires hanging, a few of those jellyfishes floating through the air, and ceiling panels open and overflowing with cables. The scene would glitch back to the "reality" before as the teacher catches the main character lagging behind and pulls them into class.
Am I saying this ever happened to me? No. But do I find corners of rooms fascinating and find myself staring into them like the peppermint butler dude in Adventure Time leading them into the underworld? Not exactly..

5/5/22: Today necessitated laundry, and the rain lifted in the evening. Waitng 18 minutes for the dryer to ding, I found myself underground, in the warm belly of the churning laundry room. Feeling someone was always around the corner, I felt myself tittering within the space. I found two detective books sitting a top pie pans and next to some soap dispensers that had a definitively New Mexico feel. Reading the backs of the books and flipping to random pages, I found myself thrown into gritty and fast pace banter as the dectectives did more to tell people what had/was happening than rather than investigate anything. There was also an obligatory page about how things are differnt now, but this ole detective doesn't rely on technology--no sir--he does some good ole fashion surveillance and illegal witness intimidation.

I think I need to branch out of my wheelhouse of genres. There is something to be said about diving into a supposed structure of work I only have the vaguest notions of without having ever touched any of the original material. Perhaps this is my sign to write the next great film noir style story, or just to fill shelves at grocery stores with something less abhorant but equally as tantalizing and wish-fulfilling as these detective novels.
